According to Actor-Network Theory (ANT), developed by Bruno Latour and his colleagues in the early 1980s, every entity within the social and natural world exists within constantly shifting networks of relationships. This perspective implies that a place is never static; its characteristics are always in flux, shaped by the dynamic interplay of various factors. The concept of 'streetscape' is introduced to capture and monitor these shifts and changes, focusing on the relationships that define a space. Specifically, the project focuses on Bergmannstrasse in the Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg district of Berlin. It uses a timeline to represent social events and political decisions that affect the street. In addition, an interactive map is provided to explore the social, cultural and economic richness of the street. The project also incorporates photographic documentation and demographic research to provide a comprehensive overview of Bergmannstrasse. Notably, the project was developed with the help of ChatGPT. For a deeper understanding, viewers are encouraged to consult the project's documentation page.
